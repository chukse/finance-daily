# Daily Market Brief — 2026-06-22

**TL;DR:** Mega-cap tech cracked on hawkish Fed bets and Iran oil relief, while the Dow and small-caps held up on rotation.

---

## What Moved Markets

**Rate fears dominate the tone.** Bank of America now expects three Fed hikes this year, citing inflation that's "unambiguously worse." The 10Y yield climbed 1.26% to 4.51%, VIX jumped 6.95% to 17.5, and the Nasdaq bore the brunt, off 1.18% while the rate-sensitive Dow eked out +0.37%.

**Iran oil sanctions pause crushed crude.** Treasury authorized Iranian oil sales through August as nuclear talks advance. WTI dropped 4.09% to $73.47, its sharpest single-day drop in weeks. Gold slipped 0.65% to $4,197 in sympathy with the risk-off tone in commodities.

**Rotation into cyclicals/small-caps.** The Russell 2000 gained 0.67%, consistent with a value/cyclical tilt as growth sold off. Dollar was essentially flat at 101.01.

**Bitcoin caught a bid**, up 2.21% to $64,635 (ETH +2.08%), partially absorbing flows out of large-cap tech.
