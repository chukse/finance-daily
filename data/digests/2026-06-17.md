# Daily Market Brief — 2026-06-17

**Daily Brief — Wednesday, June 17, 2026**

**TL;DR:** Markets tread water ahead of the first Fed rate decision under new Chair Kevin Warsh; Dow hits a record while mega-cap tech drags the Nasdaq slightly negative.

---

## What Moved Markets

**Rate decision in focus.** The FOMC decision is today's centerpiece. Kalshi traders see a more unified board under Warsh, though headlines describe the committee as structurally hawkish. The 10Y yield fell 5.3 bps to 4.43%, the sharpest move on the day, suggesting bond markets are leaning toward a hold with a dovish tilt possible.

**Dow to a record; small-caps lead.** The Dow +0.33% to 52,170 set a new high, driven by cyclicals. The Russell 2000 outperformed at +0.89%, consistent with the rate-sensitive small-cap bid triggered by the falling yield. S&P and Nasdaq were essentially flat.

**Gold at $4,380, up 1.1%.** The rally tracks the softer dollar-yield environment and hedging ahead of the Fed. WTI crude +0.6% to $76.54.

**Big-tech weakness.** GOOGL -2.1%, AMZN -1.9%, MSFT -1.8%, and TSLA -1.2% weighed on the Nasdaq. MSFT's Azure growth disappointed; Alphabet faces ongoing AI competition pressure.

**Crypto soft.** BTC -0.8% to $65,062; ETH -2.1% to $1,753. Binance reports of an impending European operating license loss added sector pressure.
