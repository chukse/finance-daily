# Daily Market Brief — 2026-06-24

**TL;DR:** Oil crashed 4.3% on Hormuz reassurances while large-cap tech lifted the S&P to 7,397; small-caps lagged badly.

## What Moved Markets

**Oil & Geopolitics:** WTI fell $3.17 to $70.04, its sharpest single-day drop in months, after Trump said Iran assured the U.S. there would be no Hormuz tolls. Brent hit its lowest level since before the Iran war began. Physical crude markets are already awash in discounts as Middle East producers ramp supply.

**Equities — split tape:** S&P 500 +0.42% and Nasdaq +0.50% recovered from yesterday's chip-led sell-off, but the Russell 2000 dropped 0.96%, signaling the rally remains concentrated in mega-cap tech. VIX eased to 19.2.

**Rates & Dollar:** 10Y yield fell 2 bps to 4.43%, providing mild support for growth stocks. Dollar ticked up 0.28% to 101.70.

**Gold:** Dropped 3.4% to $3,990, hit simultaneously by a firmer dollar and traders pricing in a more hawkish Fed. The round-number $4,000 floor did not hold.

**Bitcoin/Crypto:** BTC slipped 1.4% to $61,786; ETH -0.6%. Both opened at two-week lows with no specific catalyst beyond risk-off pressure in the alternative-asset space.
