# Daily Market Brief — 2026-06-25

**TL;DR:** Micron's blowout earnings lifted chips and offset a hotter-than-expected PCE print that pushed gold to $4,036 and sent Apple down 4% on its own price hikes.

---

## What Moved Markets

**Broad indices:** All four major indices gained, with the Dow leading (+0.69%) and VIX dropping 4.2% to 17.85—suggesting the inflation data didn't panic traders as much as the initial read might suggest.

**Inflation data (the key tension):** May PCE core came in at 3.4%, the highest since October 2023, with the headline measure topping 4.0% per Reuters. That keeps a rate hike back on the table and weighed on the 10Y yield—counterintuitively, yields *fell* 1.5% to 4.38%, possibly on the GDP surprise softening growth fears alongside the inflation print.

**Micron (+19%):** Blowout quarterly earnings drove the headline chip rally. Wedbush says the move has further to run. QQQ outperformed the Nasdaq composite significantly (+1.68% vs. +0.44%), reflecting concentrated chip/AI gains masking weakness in mega-cap tech.

**Gold:** +1.14% to $4,036—a record-area print driven by the hot inflation data and residual dollar softness (DXY -0.10% to 101.51).

**Oil:** WTI slipped another 0.37% to $70.08; cheaper gasoline is providing some consumer cushion against inflation.

**Bitcoin:** Hit multi-year lows intraday; closed at $60,770 (-0.37%). Traders are positioned for further downside per CNBC.
