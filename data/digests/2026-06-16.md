# Daily Market Brief — 2026-06-16

**TL;DR:** A US-Iran deal reopening the Strait of Hormuz cratered crude 7%, slashing inflation fears and sending stocks sharply higher — except chips, which partially rolled over.

---

## What Moved Markets

**Oil collapse was the day's engine.** WTI fell $5.69 to $75.06 (-7.05%) and Brent dipped below $80 for the first time since March on hopes an interim US-Iran deal will restore Hormuz transit. Lower energy prices repriced the inflation path lower, pulling the 10Y yield down 6 bps to 4.426% and crushing VIX by nearly 10% to 15.94.

**Equities rallied broadly but unevenly.** The Dow gained 450+ points (+1.86%) and S&P 500 added 1.54%, both straightforward beneficiaries of the oil/inflation trade. The Nasdaq's +2.64% headline masks a split session — mega-cap internet names surged while a chip rollover weighed on QQQ, which actually *fell* 1.10% even as the Nasdaq composite rose, pointing to index-composition distortion from concentrated chip selling.

**Russell 2000 lagged (+0.46%)**, underperforming large-caps despite the macro tailwind, suggesting small-cap credit concerns aren't fully resolved.

**Dollar softened modestly** (-0.25% to 99.50), consistent with reduced safe-haven demand. Gold added 0.72% to $4,359, holding near record territory. Bitcoin slipped 0.74% to $65,802.

**Fed meeting began today** — no rate decision expected; Kevin Warsh's FOMC seen on hold "for a while" per CNBC survey.

**Hormuz shipping floor remains.** Spot oil premiums slipped on the Iran deal but haven't collapsed — tanker operators still pricing in uncertainty. Fertilizer supply chains may be more stranded than crude itself.
