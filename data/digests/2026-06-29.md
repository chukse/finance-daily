# Daily Market Brief — 2026-06-29

**TL;DR:** U.S.-Iran ceasefire lifts equities and crude, with mega-cap growth leading while small-caps and gold lag.

---

## What moved markets

**Geopolitics drove the tape.** After four days of strikes, Washington and Tehran agreed to halt hostilities and resume talks in Qatar Tuesday. The headline pushed S&P 500 +0.74% to 7,409 and Nasdaq +1.22% to 25,605, though gains were capped by selective selling in chips.

**Oil recovered sharply.** WTI +1.78% to $70.46, back above the psychologically significant $70 level. Analysts flagged persistent supply-risk premium even as prices approach pre-conflict levels.

**Safe-haven unwind.** Gold shed 1.10% to $4,034 as risk appetite returned. The dollar slipped 0.15% (DXY 101.21) while the yen hit a 40-year low. 10Y Treasury yield edged up 0.9 bps to 4.376%.

**Small-caps disconnected.** Russell 2000 -0.62% against broad-market gains — consistent with a risk rally driven by macro relief rather than domestic growth optimism. VIX ticked up slightly to 18.5, suggesting residual caution.

## Stories worth knowing

**Comcast +9%:** Board approved spinning off NBCUniversal and Sky from the cable business — latest major media unbundling. Shares pared gains into close as investors parsed execution risk.

**Rocket Lab / Iridium:** RKLB agreed to acquire satellite operator Iridium in an $8B deal, a direct challenge to SpaceX's Starlink dominance in low-earth-orbit connectivity.

**Fed independence:** Supreme Court blocked Trump's attempt to fire Fed Governor Lisa Cook — for now — while allowing removals at other independent agencies. Ruling keeps the Fed question in legal limbo.

**Martin Marietta / Lhoist:** $13.5B acquisition of Lhoist North America, a major lime and minerals producer — significant consolidation in construction materials.

**JPMorgan succession:** Reuters reports internal signals suggest CEO succession planning is advancing in earnest; Dimon's eventual exit now viewed as near-term rather than abstract.

**Trump / Axon:** CNBC reports Trump purchased up to $5M in Axon stock before ICE sought a $220M Taser contract — conflict-of-interest scrutiny likely to follow.
