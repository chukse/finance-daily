# Daily Market Brief — 2026-06-23

**TL;DR:** A global chip rout triggered by hawkish Fed fears and cooling AI sentiment sent the Nasdaq down 1.2%, with the VIX spiking nearly 14% and SpaceX falling below its IPO debut price.

---

## What Moved Markets

**Chip/AI selloff** was the day's dominant force. Micron, Nvidia (-2.5%), and broader semis led equity declines as Goldman Sachs flagged stretched AI trade assumptions and Reuters cited mounting concern over AI spending returns. Nasdaq fell 1.23%; QQQ dropped 2.15%. Russell 2000 (-1.42%) underperformed, signaling broad risk-off.

**SpaceX** extended losses, falling below its $150 IPO debut price and erasing over $600 billion in market cap from peak. Retail investors who piled into the trading frenzy are now underwater.

**Fed fears** amplified the move. Rate-hike language circulating in headlines pushed the 10Y yield up 3.4 bps to 4.485% and hammered gold (-0.66%) and silver simultaneously — unusual pressure on traditional safe havens. VIX at 19.68 is back in elevated territory.

**Oil** dropped 2.2% to $73.17 on subdued Strait of Hormuz disruption risk and soft demand signals.

**Bitcoin** fell 2.3% to $62,473; ETH down 3.7% — tracking the risk-off move in tech.

**KOSPI** plunged ~10% after South Korea's regulator cautioned on leveraged ETFs — a notable external stress signal.

**China** slapped restrictions on dozens of U.S. companies (WSJ), adding a geopolitical undercurrent to tech weakness.
