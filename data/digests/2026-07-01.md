# Daily Market Brief — 2026-07-01

**Daily Brief — July 1, 2026**

**TL;DR:** Soft ADP jobs data and a hawkish Fed Chair Warsh pushed yields higher and equities lower to start H2, while gold surged and NVDA dragged tech.

---

## What Moved Markets

**Rates & Fed:** The dominant force. The 10Y Treasury yield jumped 2.40% to 4.477%, after Fed Chair Kevin Warsh explicitly refused to signal a July cut and flagged inflation as "too high." The VIX rose 4.44% to 17.18, reflecting the repricing of rate expectations.

**Jobs miss:** ADP private payrolls came in at 98K for June, well below consensus. Adds to a picture of a softening but not collapsing labor market — not soft enough for the Fed to pivot.

**Equities:** Nasdaq fell 0.67%, underperforming as rate-sensitive tech sold off. S&P 500 down 0.34%, Dow nearly flat (-0.08%). The QQQ shed 0.98%. Narrow leadership remains a concern — MarketWatch notes almost all of Nasdaq-100's H1 gains came from just 10 stocks.

**Gold:** Up 1.82% to $4,096 — a safe-haven bid into H2 despite the strong dollar, even after its worst quarter in 13 years.

**Oil:** WTI fell 0.81% to $68.94 as US-Iran technical talks in Qatar progressed, raising expectations of eventual supply resumption through the Strait of Hormuz.

**Dollar/Bitcoin:** DXY barely moved (+0.09%). BTC gained 1.31% to $59,323; ETH up 1.46% — modest risk appetite in crypto even as equities weakened.
