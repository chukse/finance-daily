# Daily Market Brief — 2026-06-18

**TL;DR:** US-Iran ceasefire deal crushed oil 4.8% and gold 2.1% while sparking a broad equity rally; Intel surged on a Trump-announced Apple chip partnership.

---

## What moved markets

**Equities up, risk-off assets down.** S&P 500 +0.73% to 7,474, Nasdaq +0.96%, Russell 2000 +0.75%. VIX dropped 5.5% to 17.4, signaling the prior session's Fed-driven anxiety fading.

**Iran ceasefire is the macro pivot.** A signed US-Iran ceasefire deal sent WTI crude to $73.11 (-4.8%), its lowest since before the Iran war began. Gold fell $91 to $4,267 (-2.1%). Bitcoin -2.9% to $63,700; Ethereum -3.4% to $1,730 — both tracking the broad risk-off unwind in safe-haven and alternative assets.

**Rates and dollar.** 10Y Treasury yield slipped to 4.43% (-1.2%) as geopolitical risk premium compressed. Dollar Index firmed +0.6% to 100.64, likely absorbing flows out of energy and commodities.

**Fed overhang persists.** Tuesday's hawkish Fed session still in the backdrop; Bank of England held rates steady, citing Iran truce uncertainty as a reason for caution.
