# Daily Market Brief — 2026-06-26

**TL;DR:** Tech and chips sold off on an OpenAI IPO delay report, pushing Nasdaq down 1% while small-caps bucked the trend and gold hit $4,070.

---

## What Moved Markets

**Chip/AI weakness led the decline.** NVDA fell 1.67% and the Nasdaq dropped 0.99% after a report that OpenAI's IPO is being delayed, damping AI-adjacent sentiment. Micron fell ~6% in premarket (paring earlier post-earnings gains) amid the broader tech rout.

**Small-caps diverged sharply.** Russell 2000 +0.71% vs. S&P 500 -0.53% — a classic rotation into value/cyclicals as mega-cap tech shed weight. The spread is notable.

**VIX jumped 7.3% to 20.27**, signaling a meaningful uptick in hedging demand. First weekly equity fund outflows since March reinforce a risk-off tone heading into summer.

**Oil broke below $70.** WTI -2.84% to $69.88 despite a cargo ship being struck near Oman in the Strait of Hormuz. The underlying pressure: a MarketWatch piece notes a world increasingly rejecting OPEC+ discipline could push prices toward $50. The geopolitical spike failed to hold.

**Gold +0.98% to $4,070** — haven demand alongside dollar weakness (DXY -0.33% to 101.10) and easing inflation fears pulling Treasury yields lower (10Y at 4.40%).

**Bitcoin slid 1.28% to $58,956**, Ethereum -1.85% to $1,536 — tracking risk-off in tech.

## Stories Worth Knowing

**OpenAI IPO delay** is the market-moving headline. No new timeline disclosed; uncertainty is weighing on the entire AI infrastructure trade.

**Strait of Hormuz tension, not priced in.** Iran reasserted control rights over the waterway after a cargo ship was struck near Oman. Oil sold off anyway — reflecting demand pessimism overriding supply-risk premium, but a sustained disruption would reprice fast.

**Volkswagen cutting 15% of workforce, closing four German plants.** Structural collapse of European auto continues; no positive spin here.

**Trump clean energy tax credit deadline** is forcing a rush of renewable project completions before cutoff — a short-term capex surge with a hard stop.

**SK Hynix making its Nasdaq debut** — the Korean memory giant listing stateside is worth tracking given memory chip cost pressures flagged across the laptop/smartphone retail chain.

**US equity funds saw first weekly outflows since March** — institutional repositioning, not just retail jitters. Watch whether next week's jobs data reverses or accelerates that flow.
