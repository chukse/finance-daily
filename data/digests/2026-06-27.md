# Daily Market Brief — 2026-06-27

**TL;DR:** U.S. strikes Iran over Hormuz ceasefire violations dominate the session, sending oil sharply lower on resumed Saudi supply while gold jumps and tech rotates hard.

## What Moved Markets

**Geopolitics/Oil:** The dominant story. U.S. struck Iran after Trump accused Tehran of violating ceasefire terms in the Strait of Hormuz; Iran's Revolutionary Guards retaliated against "U.S.-linked targets." Despite the escalation, WTI crude fell -3.74% to $69.23 — Saudi Aramco resumed loading at Ras Tanura, offsetting supply-disruption fears. Gold rose +1.20% to $4,079, its safe-haven bid intact.

**Equities:** Broad indices nearly flat (S&P -0.05%, Dow -0.09%) but the surface calm masks rotation. Russell 2000 edged +0.07%, outperforming large-caps. VIX fell -2.54% to 18.41 despite geopolitical noise, suggesting the market is not panicking on Iran.

**Rates/Dollar:** 10Y yield at 4.37%. Dollar index slipped -0.07% to 101.36 — no flight-to-dollar on the Iran news, notable.

**Semis/Tech split:** Memory shortage described as an "existential crisis" for smaller players; NVDA -1.64%, GOOGL -1.84% dragged Nasdaq -0.24%. Mega-cap software diverged sharply higher (see Watchlist).
