# Daily Market Brief — 2026-06-14

**Daily Brief — June 14, 2026**

**TL;DR:** A U.S.-Iran peace deal sent oil tumbling 5.4% and gold surging 3.3% while equities and crypto rallied broadly on hopes the Strait of Hormuz reopens.

---

## What Moved Markets

**The deal.** Trump announced a preliminary U.S.-Iran agreement to end the Mideast war; signing is set for Friday. That single headline drove virtually every notable move today.

**Equities** broadly higher: S&P 500 +0.50% to 7,431, Dow +0.70% to 51,202, Russell 2000 led at +0.79% (risk-on rotation into small caps), Nasdaq lagged at +0.31%. VIX dropped sharply, down 9.1% to 17.68.

**Oil** was the biggest loser — WTI -5.4% to $80.27. Hormuz reopening removes the supply disruption premium that had been built in. WSJ noted Brent could fall to $80 by year-end if the strait stays open.

**Gold** +3.3% to $4,352.50 — counterintuitively strong; likely reflecting continued dollar skepticism and uncertainty that the deal actually closes Friday, as some investors cautioned it isn't signed yet.

**10Y Treasury yield** ticked up to 4.49% (+0.54%), consistent with improved risk appetite reducing safe-haven demand.

**Dollar** slipped 0.18% to 99.57, hitting a 10-day low per Reuters; deal removes some geopolitical haven demand for USD.

**Bitcoin/Crypto** joined the rally: BTC +1.8% to $65,600; ETH +2.4% to $1,721.

## Stories Worth Knowing

- **Deal risk:** Multiple outlets noted the agreement is preliminary and unsigned. Markets are pricing a done deal; if Friday's signing slips, oil and VIX could reverse hard.
- **Kevin Warsh/Fed:** Ahead of his first meeting as Fed chair, economists say they genuinely don't know his policy lean. That uncertainty is worth watching as the next rate decision approaches.
- **Space investing:** WSJ reports retail and institutional money is piling into space-sector plays emboldened by SpaceX's momentum — a sector rotation story developing beneath the Iran headlines.
- **Anthropic in D.C.:** Staff dispatched to resolve AI export restrictions, suggesting the regulatory fight over AI chip/model exports is intensifying at the policy level.
- **European sanctions relief:** UK, France, Germany, and Italy all signaled readiness to lift Iran sanctions post-deal, which would add Iranian barrels to global supply — another medium-term headwind for oil prices.
